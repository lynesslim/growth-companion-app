# Product Requirements Document: Daily Growth Companion App

## 1. Product Summary

This app is a personalised growth companion that helps users become the person they want to be through daily learning, action, and visible progress.

Instead of functioning as a traditional book summary app, the product recommends books and extracts only the most relevant insights based on the user’s personal goals, interests, current challenges, and desired identity.

For each recommended book, the app gives users:

1. A short explanation of what the book is about based on the user’s goal
2. Three personalised lessons from the book
3. A suggested chapter to read first
4. One simple daily action
5. A trackable task inside the user’s Growth Quest List

The core experience is not “read more books.”

The core experience is:

**Choose who you want to become. Learn one useful thing daily. Take one small action. Track your growth. Watch your companion evolve with you.**

The product combines personalised learning, task-based action, habit-building, gamified progress, and a collectible companion system to make reading feel cool, visible, and identity-driven — similar to how Strava made running social and aspirational.

The app aims to reposition reading from something “nerdy” or “old structured people do” into something users feel proud to show.

## 4. Target Users

### Primary target user

Gen Z and young millennial users who want to grow personally, professionally, socially, or creatively, but do not identify with traditional book summary apps.

They want to become smarter, more confident, more successful, more interesting, or more disciplined, but they need the experience to feel lightweight, aesthetic, personalised, and rewarding.

### User examples

2. A founder or aspiring entrepreneur who wants to improve business thinking.

## 5. Problem Statement

Reading is valuable, but it does not currently feel cool, visible, or emotionally rewarding for younger users.

Existing book summary apps mainly solve the utility problem:

“How can I consume book ideas faster?”

But they do not solve the identity and behaviour problem:

“Why should I care today?”
“How does this help me become who I want to be?”
“What should I actually do after learning this?”
“How do I track whether I am improving?”
“How do I show that I am growing?”

This app solves that by turning reading into a personalised growth journey made up of daily insights, daily actions, tracked tasks, and visible companion evolution.

## 11. Companion System

The companion is not merely decorative. It represents the user’s growth identity.

### Companion concept

Users choose one of three starter companions during onboarding. Each companion is creature-like, collectible, cute, mature, and inspired by designer toy aesthetics.

The companion should not look too childish, too humanoid, or too game-like. It should feel like something an adult would like to collect, share, and emotionally attach to.

### Starter companion archetypes

1. Ambition Companion

   * Represents discipline, career, money, business, action, leadership.
   * Best for users who want to become more successful, productive, or confident.

2. Creativity Companion

   * Represents expression, storytelling, imagination, originality, branding, content.
   * Best for users who want to become more creative or visible.

3. Wisdom Companion

   * Represents focus, clarity, mindset, calmness, psychology, decision-making.
   * Best for users who want to become more thoughtful, focused, or emotionally steady.

### Companion growth rules

The companion gains XP when the user:

* Opens the daily growth drop
* Reads the personalised lessons
* Completes the daily action
* Checks in
* Maintains streaks
* Completes tasks from the Growth Quest List
* Explores recommended chapters
* Reflects on what they learned

### Evolution logic

The companion evolves based on the user’s learning and action pattern.

For example:

* Business and leadership content increases Strategy XP
* Creativity and storytelling content increases Creativity XP
* Productivity and habit content increases Discipline XP
* Psychology and mindset content increases Wisdom XP
* Communication content increases Influence XP
* Money and finance content increases Wealth XP

The companion’s visual evolution should reflect the user’s dominant growth categories.

The app recommends one primary book per day based on the user’s goal, interests, and progress.

### Recommendation output

1. Book title

4. Short goal-based summary

## 13. Book Insight Format

Each recommended book should be displayed in a modern, lightweight, Gen Z-friendly format.

### Section 1: Why this book for you

This section explains why the book is relevant based on the user’s objective.

Example:

“You said you want to become more focused and consistent. This book is useful because it explains why deep, distraction-free work is becoming rare, and why people who can protect their attention have an advantage.”

### Section 2: What this book is about

This is a short, personalised overview of the book.

It should not be a generic back-cover description. It should connect the book to the user’s goal.

Example:

“This book is about training your ability to focus in a distracted world. For your goal of becoming more disciplined, the key idea is not just time management — it is learning how to protect your attention and create conditions where serious work becomes easier.”

### Section 3: Three lessons for you

The app extracts three lessons that are most relevant to the user.

Each lesson should include:

* Lesson title
* Short explanation
* Why it matters to the user
* Optional example

Example:

Lesson 1: Your attention is your competitive advantage
Lesson 2: Shallow work feels productive but often is not
Lesson 3: Focus improves when your environment removes friction

### Section 4: Chapter to read first

Instead of telling the user to read the whole book, the app recommends the most relevant chapter to begin with.

Example:

“Start with Chapter 3 because it gives you the clearest practical framework for creating deep work rituals. Since your main struggle is distraction, this chapter gives you the fastest starting point.”

### Section 5: Today’s action

The app gives one simple action based on the lesson.

Example:

“Block 30 minutes today for one important task. Put your phone away, close unnecessary tabs, and work on only that task. After that, check in and tell your companion how it went.”

## 14. Growth Quest List / Task System

The app should include a central task list that tracks all actions generated from book recommendations and the user’s growth path.

This should not feel like a generic productivity task list. It should feel like a personal growth quest tracker.

### Purpose

The Growth Quest List exists to turn reading into behaviour.

Without this feature, the app risks becoming another passive content app. With this feature, the app becomes a system that helps users learn, act, track, reflect, and grow.

### Core principle

The app should give users only one primary required task per day.

The user should feel:

“I only need to do one small thing today to keep growing.”

The app should not make the user feel overwhelmed by a long to-do list.

### Main task sections

#### 1. Today

Shows the user’s main daily action.

This is the most important task for the day and should be visually prioritised.

Example:

“Protect 30 minutes of focus.”

Inspired by: Deep Work
Goal: Become more disciplined
Reward: +10 Focus XP
Estimated time: 30 minutes

#### 2. Upcoming

Shows upcoming or saved growth tasks generated from the user’s growth path.

These are optional and should not create pressure.

#### 3. Completed

Shows completed tasks, including:

* Task title
* Date completed
* Related book
* Related goal
* XP earned
* User reflection
* Companion growth impact

#### 4. Skipped / Rescheduled

Shows tasks the user skipped or moved.

This section helps the app learn whether the task was too difficult, irrelevant, too time-consuming, or poorly timed.

### Task card requirements

Each task card should include:

* Task title
* Short description
* Estimated time
* Related goal
* Related book
* Related lesson
* XP reward
* Status
* Completion button
* Skip button
* Optional reflection prompt

### Task statuses

Each task can have one of the following statuses:

* Pending
* Completed
* Partially completed
* Skipped
* Rescheduled
* Expired

### Task creation rules

Tasks can be created from:

1. Daily book recommendation
2. AI-generated growth plan
3. User-saved lessons
4. Missed or rescheduled tasks
5. Premium personalised plan

### Task completion flow

1. User reads daily growth drop
2. User sees today’s action
3. User taps “Add to Today” or the task is automatically added
4. Task appears in Today’s Growth Quest List
5. User completes, skips, or reschedules it
6. User optionally writes a short reflection
7. Companion gains XP
8. Task moves to Completed
9. Future recommendations adjust based on behaviour

### Reflection prompt examples

“What did you notice?”
“What made this easy or hard?”
“How did this help your future self?”
“Would you do this again?”
“Was this too easy, too hard, or just right?”

### Personalisation value

The task system should feed back into the recommendation engine.

If a user keeps completing focus tasks, the app can recommend deeper focus-related books or harder actions.

If a user keeps skipping communication tasks, the app can ask whether the task feels uncomfortable, irrelevant, or too difficult.

If a user completes many creativity tasks, the companion should gain Creativity XP and visually evolve in that direction.

## 24. Content Strategy

The app will use an **AI-First Content Strategy**, meaning it does not rely on a static, manually curated library of books.

### AI Generation

Instead of pre-writing summaries and tasks, the app will leverage large language models (like OpenAI GPT-4o) to dynamically generate content.

For each user's Daily Growth Drop, the AI will:
1. Search its vast internal knowledge base for a relevant, real-world non-fiction book that addresses the user's specific goal and current struggle.
2. Dynamically extract the three most relevant lessons.
3. Formulate a simple, context-aware daily action.

### Advantages for MVP

* **Zero Content Overhead:** No need to build a CMS or spend weeks writing book summaries.
* **Infinite Library:** The app can recommend niche or obscure books tailored exactly to a user's unique struggle.
* **Cheaper & Faster MVP:** Less backend code and fewer database tables to maintain.

*(Note: The prompt to the AI must strongly enforce that it only recommends real, published books and does not hallucinate titles.)*

## 28. Key Risks

### Risk 1: The app feels like a normal book summary app

Mitigation: Do not lead with summaries. Lead with growth drops, identity, and action.

### Risk 2: The companion feels childish

Mitigation: Use mature collectible creature aesthetics, not children’s cartoon or RPG visuals.

### Risk 3: Users consume but do not act

Mitigation: Make the daily action and Growth Quest List central to the experience.

### Risk 4: The task list feels like boring productivity software

Mitigation: Frame tasks as Growth Quests, keep the daily requirement to one task, and connect every task to XP, companion growth, and identity.

### Risk 5: Personalisation feels fake

Mitigation: Use onboarding data, task completion, skipped tasks, and reflections to generate clearly user-specific outputs.

### Risk 6: Users do not return daily

Mitigation: Use streaks, companion progress, daily drops, reminders, weekly recaps, and incomplete quest nudges.

### Risk 7: Content licensing becomes complicated

Mitigation: Start with original commentary, public metadata, and goal-based recommendations. Avoid presenting the app as a replacement for the book.

## 31. Final Product Definition

This app is a personalised growth companion that makes reading feel like leveling up.

It does not simply summarise books. It recommends the right book at the right time, explains why it matters to the user, extracts the most relevant lessons, suggests where to start reading, and turns the insight into one small daily action.

The Growth Quest List makes the user’s actions trackable. It gives structure to the journey and shows that the user is not just consuming knowledge, but applying it.

The companion system makes progress visible and emotionally rewarding. Over time, the user’s companion evolves based on what they read, what they do, and who they are becoming.

The product’s ultimate goal is to make reading and self-improvement feel cool, social, collectible, and identity-driven for a younger generation.
